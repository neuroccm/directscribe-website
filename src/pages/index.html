Precise about locality

What runs where

We describe "local-first" precisely. Your files, templates, keys, and transparency log live on your Mac. But at launch, turning speech into text always uses a cloud transcription vendor you choose — so we say so plainly instead of implying that your audio is processed only on your Mac.

The one step that can run entirely on your Mac today is note-shaping — natively with Apple Intelligence (on-device), or with a local model via LM Studio.

Built to outlast your EMR

Speech-to-text and AI move faster than EMRs ever will

EMRs are data repositories for clinical care — not places where cutting-edge transcription or language models show up first. DirectScribe stays deliberately separate from your EMR: dictate, review, paste. That gap is the point.

Your vendors evolve. DirectScribe doesn't lock you to any of them.

When a better or cheaper transcription or intelligence vendor arrives, you switch providers in Settings — no waiting on your EMR vendor to catch up, no migration.

Your EMR stays the system of record — not a bottleneck.

The finished note pastes in as plain text. Your EMR keeps doing what it's good at: being the durable record of clinical care, while DirectScribe stays free to move as fast as the AI landscape does.

Security by design

The design is the security story

Not a bolt-on. The product is shaped so that the sensitive data has nowhere unexpected to go.

  • Your dictation, not the room You dictate into your mic and DirectScribe captures your voice. It isn't an ambient scribe — there's no microphone left open on the visit.
  • No DirectScribe servers Your PHI never touches our servers, because there are none. You send it straight to the vendors you chose.
  • Keys in the macOS Keychain API keys are stored by the system Keychain, never in plain files and never in the cloud.
  • Sandboxed app DirectScribe runs in the macOS App Sandbox with least-privilege access to your system.
  • Deletion on your schedule Session-end and 7/30/90-day sweeps remove recordings, transcripts, and notes on the schedule you set.
  • Tamper-evident transparency log A hash-chained, sequence-checkpointed record of every transmission and deletion — PHI-free by design.
  • Offline licensing A license key validated on your Mac. No account, no sign-in, no server that can lock you out.

Deletion, stated honestly

Deleted means deleted from the file system on your schedule; FileVault protects residual storage; backups you configure may retain copies. We don't claim data disappears from every disk — we tell you exactly what deletion does, and the transparency log proves the action.
